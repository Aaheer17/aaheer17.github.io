About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, working at the Biocomplexity Institute under the supervision of Prof. Geoffrey C. Fox. My research focuses on using scientific machine learning and generative models to build fast, reliable surrogates for complex scientific simulations, especially calorimeter shower simulation in high-energy physics.

I am particularly interested in how diffusion and flow-matching models can be adapted, fine-tuned, and evaluated so that they preserve key physics structures and remain trustworthy when used in real analysis pipelines.

Research areas

Research

My current work centers on physics-guided diffusion models for calorimeter showers and on systematic benchmarking of generative models for scientific data.

Current focus

  • LANTERN: Physics-Guided Diffusion for Calorimeter Showers
    Base diffusion objective augmented with physics-guided auxiliary losses (e.g., correlation-aware CFD, MVN likelihood, energy consistency) and timestep weighting/warm-up.
  • LANTERN → LEMURS Adaptation
    Porting LANTERN to the multi-detector LEMURS dataset with additional conditioning (incident energy, θ/ϕ angles) and diffusion fine-tuning for transfer across heterogeneous calorimeter geometries.
  • CaloBench: Benchmarking Generative Models for Calorimeter Data
    Multi-detector evaluation of diffusion/score-based and flow-matching models on calorimeter showers; introduced the Correlation Frobenius Distance (CFD) metric to quantify preservation of second-order structure.

Selected Projects

Professional projects

  • LANTERN: Physics-Guided Diffusion for Calorimeter Showers
    Physics-guided diffusion surrogate for fast calorimeter simulation with auxiliary CFD/MVN/energy objectives and timestep-aware training.
  • LANTERN → LEMURS Adaptation
    Adapting LANTERN to the LEMURS dataset with extra geometric and angular conditions to study cross-detector generalization.
  • CaloBench
    Benchmarking diffusion, score-based, and flow-matching models across multiple calorimeter datasets; reproducible configs and evaluation code.
  • Reinforcement Learning for Dynamic Task Scheduling
    Efficient dynamic task scheduling in heterogeneous environments using reinforcement learning.

Academic projects

  • Mixture-of-Experts for Fake News Detection
    A Mixture-of-Experts deep learning model for fake news detection, comparing MoE against baselines such as SVM and BERT.
  • PCV – A Point-Cloud-Based Network Verifier
    Point-cloud based verifier that evaluates robustness of a 3D classifier (PointNet) by generating adversarial inputs.
  • Remote DNS Cache Poisoning (Kaminsky Attack)
    Implemented a remote DNS cache poisoning technique following the Kaminsky attack.
  • Catch Me if I’m Angry
    Human-Computer Interaction project to detect anger in email content and gently discourage users from sending messages written in anger.

Personal projects

  • PhinD-me-if-You-Can (Claude for Good 2025 – Student Track)
    LLM-based tool to help students discover potential PhD advisors by matching research interests; built as a 7.5-hour Streamlit prototype.
  • Calo-RAG: Literature Retrieval for Calorimeter Modeling
    Small RAG pipeline with prompt templates, citation-aware retrieval, and basic reranking; evaluated coverage using precision/recall on a curated query set.
  • LLM Time-Series Forecasting for Conflict-Induced Migration (learning)
    Prompt-based daily forecasts with exogenous conflict/fatality inputs and rolling-window RMSE; planned comparison to an agent-based migration model.
